The prevailing idea is that once the antimatter comes into contact with matter, both the Vatican and Rome would vanish. The Illuminati (the antagonists) send the police a live feed of the ticking bomb. Camerlengo, the priestly manager of the Vatican (played by Ewan McGregor), suggests to the police that they should turn off the city's electricity, block by block, to identify the source of the live feed. These constant shutdowns create challenges for Professor Langdon (Tom Hanks) and the scientist assisting him. Upon watching the movie, I pondered: Why turn off the city lights block by block? That's a sequential algorithm! A Binary Search would be much more efficient. Let's assume, for instance, that Rome has 64 blocks. Following the movie's sequential approach, there would be 64 blackouts. If each blackout lasts 1 minute, it would take more than one hour to find the bomb.
